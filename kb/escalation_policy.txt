ESCALATION POLICY — Campaign Manager Support

=======================================================
SECTION 1: ESCALATION CONDITIONS
=======================================================

A support case is escalated from AI/Tier-1 to a human agent when any of the following conditions are met:

Mandatory Escalation Triggers:
1. Refund Demand: Customer explicitly requests a refund for any amount (regardless of eligibility)
2. Legal Threat: Customer mentions legal action, lawyers, regulatory complaints (FTC, GDPR DPAs), or lawsuits
3. Chargeback/Dispute: Customer has filed or threatens to file a payment dispute with their bank
4. Explicit Human Request: Customer asks to speak with a human agent, manager, supervisor, or account manager
5. Repeated Unresolved Issue: Same issue reported 3+ times without resolution, or customer has been waiting >24 hours without update
6. Enterprise Customer Complaint: Any complaint from a customer on an Enterprise contract
7. SLA Breach Claim: Customer reports a suspected SLA breach and requests credit
8. Data Privacy Concern: Customer raises a concern about data breach, GDPR/CCPA compliance, or requests data deletion
9. Account Closure Request: Customer requests to permanently close/delete their account and all data

Discretionary Escalation Triggers (Tier-1 agent judgement):
- High emotional distress or extreme frustration with multiple ALL-CAPS messages or profanity
- Complex technical issue requiring engineering team investigation (e.g., suspected platform bug)
- Billing dispute involving more than $500 in charges
- Media or PR sensitivity (customer identifies themselves as journalist, regulator, or influencer)

=======================================================
SECTION 2: PRIORITY CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM
=======================================================

Priority: Critical
- Definition: Immediate business-stopping impact; legal exposure or high-value enterprise at risk
- Triggers: Legal threats, Enterprise SLA breach, major financial impact (>$10,000), data breach concern
- Response Time Target: Immediate — agent assigned within 15 minutes, 24/7
- Handling Team: Senior Customer Success Manager + Legal (if required) + VP-level escalation path
- Resolution Target: First meaningful response within 15 minutes; full resolution within 4 hours

Priority: High
- Definition: Significant business impact; repeated failures or financial discrepancy
- Triggers: Refund demand, billing dispute, repeated unresolved technical issue, campaign serving completely down for >2 hours
- Response Time Target: Within 2 hours (business hours); 4 hours (outside business hours)
- Handling Team: Tier-2 Support (Senior Support Engineer or Account Manager)
- Resolution Target: Full resolution or escalation path within 24 hours

Priority: Medium
- Definition: Noticeable impact on operations; customer frustrated but business not fully stopped
- Triggers: Explicit human request, significant feature degraded, 1–2 failed resolution attempts
- Response Time Target: Within 8 business hours
- Handling Team: Tier-2 Support
- Resolution Target: Full resolution within 48 hours

Priority: Low
- Definition: General complexity or queries requiring human context; no immediate business impact
- Triggers: Complex billing question, account policy inquiry, non-urgent account changes
- Response Time Target: Within 24 business hours
- Handling Team: Tier-1 Support with Tier-2 availability
- Resolution Target: Full resolution within 5 business days

=======================================================
SECTION 3: ESCALATION WORKFLOW
=======================================================

Step-by-Step Escalation Process:
1. Escalation Triggered: AI agent or Tier-1 agent identifies escalation condition
2. Case Summary Generated: Automated summary created including: customer info, issue description, persona, priority, previous interactions
3. CRM Ticket Created: Case escalated in Salesforce/Zendesk with priority tag and full conversation transcript
4. Team Notification: Assigned team receives alert via Slack (#escalations) and email
5. Customer Acknowledgement: Automated message sent to customer: "Your case has been escalated. A [team] member will contact you within [response time]."
6. Agent Assignment: Escalation queue manager assigns to available agent based on expertise
7. Active Resolution: Human agent contacts customer via their preferred channel (email, phone, Slack)
8. Case Closure: Resolved by agent; case marked Resolved with root cause and resolution documented
9. CSAT Survey: Satisfaction survey sent 24 hours after resolution

Escalation Data Package (sent to human agent):
- Customer account details: Plan, tenure, MRR, previous support interactions
- Detected persona: Technical Expert / Frustrated User / Business Executive
- Priority level and justification
- Full conversation transcript
- Relevant KB chunks retrieved and shown to customer
- Recommended resolution path based on escalation type

=======================================================
SECTION 4: SPECIALIST ESCALATION TEAMS
=======================================================

Billing & Accounts Team:
- Handles: Refund requests, billing disputes, payment failures, invoice discrepancies
- Contact: billing-support@campaignmanager.io
- Availability: Monday–Friday 9 AM–6 PM EST

Legal & Compliance Team:
- Handles: Legal threats, GDPR/CCPA requests, chargeback investigations, regulatory inquiries
- Contact: legal@campaignmanager.io (not publicly listed; internal escalation only)
- Availability: Monday–Friday 9 AM–5 PM EST; on-call for Critical cases

Technical Engineering Team (Tier-3):
- Handles: Platform bugs, API issues requiring code investigation, data integrity issues
- Escalation path: Tier-2 → Engineering via Jira bug tracker
- Response: Within 4 hours for Critical, 1 business day for High

Enterprise Account Management:
- Handles: All Enterprise customer escalations, QBR concerns, contract disputes, SLA credit claims
- Contact: enterprise@campaignmanager.io (+ dedicated Slack channel per account)
- Availability: 24/7 for Critical; business hours for all others

Customer Success Team:
- Handles: Churn risk, sentiment recovery, feature education, onboarding issues
- Triggered when: CSAT <3/5 after resolution, account shows cancellation intent signals

=======================================================
SECTION 5: POST-ESCALATION PROCESS
=======================================================

After Resolution:
- Agent documents root cause, steps taken, and resolution in CRM
- Customer receives satisfaction survey (CSAT)
- If CSAT <3: Automatic review flagged to Customer Success Manager
- For Critical cases: Post-mortem written and reviewed by Support Director within 5 business days

Escalation Metrics Tracked:
- Time to First Response (TTFR)
- Time to Resolution (TTR)
- Escalation Rate (% of total contacts escalated)
- Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT) post-escalation
- First Contact Resolution (FCR) for similar future cases

SLA Credit Processing (if applicable):
- Finance team processes approved SLA credits within 5 business days
- Credit applied to next invoice; notification sent to billing contact
- For Enterprise financial penalties: Processed per contract terms
