SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA) & DOWNTIME POLICY — Campaign Manager Platform

=======================================================
SECTION 1: UPTIME GUARANTEE
=======================================================

Standard Uptime Commitment:
- Starter Plan: Best-effort availability (no formal SLA)
- Growth Plan: 99.5% monthly uptime guarantee
- Enterprise Plan (Standard): 99.9% monthly uptime guarantee
- Enterprise Plan (Premium): 99.99% monthly uptime guarantee

Uptime Calculation:
- Measured as a percentage of total minutes in a calendar month
- 99.9% uptime = max 43.8 minutes of allowed downtime per month
- 99.99% uptime = max 4.38 minutes of allowed downtime per month
- Uptime excludes scheduled maintenance windows (see Section 3)

What Counts as Downtime:
- API unavailability (returning 5xx errors to >5% of requests for >1 minute)
- Campaign serving failures (ads not being served to end users)
- Dashboard inaccessibility (login page or core UI not loading)
- Analytics data pipeline delays exceeding 2 hours
- What does NOT count: Client-side network issues, third-party dependency outages, scheduled maintenance

=======================================================
SECTION 2: SLA CREDITS & COMPENSATION POLICY
=======================================================

Credit Calculation Table:
- 99.0% – 99.5% uptime (Growth): 10% service credit of monthly fee
- 95.0% – 99.0% uptime (Growth): 25% service credit of monthly fee
- Below 95.0% uptime (Growth): 50% service credit of monthly fee

- 99.5% – 99.9% uptime (Enterprise Standard): 10% service credit
- 99.0% – 99.5% uptime (Enterprise Standard): 25% service credit
- Below 99.0% uptime (Enterprise Standard): 50% service credit

- 99.9% – 99.99% uptime (Enterprise Premium): 10% service credit
- 99.0% – 99.9% uptime (Enterprise Premium): 25% service credit
- Below 99.0% uptime (Enterprise Premium): 100% service credit + executive review

How to Claim SLA Credits:
1. Credits are NOT automatically applied — you must submit a claim
2. File a credit claim within 30 days of the incident using the SLA Credit Request form at billing.campaignmanager.io/sla-claim
3. Include: incident date, time, duration, and the impact on your campaigns/business
4. Claims are reviewed by the Finance team within 5 business days
5. Approved credits are applied to the next billing invoice (not issued as cash refund unless account is being cancelled)

Credit Claim Deadlines:
- Claims submitted more than 30 days after an incident are not eligible
- Only one claim per calendar month per incident category is permitted
- Credits do not accumulate across months; unused credits expire at end of billing cycle

Contract-Based Penalties (Enterprise Premium):
- Some Enterprise contracts include financial penalties (beyond service credits) for sustained outages
- Penalty triggers: >4 hours of downtime in a single incident or >6 hours total in a calendar month
- Contact your account manager or legal@campaignmanager.io for penalty assessment

=======================================================
SECTION 3: SCHEDULED MAINTENANCE WINDOWS
=======================================================

Regular Maintenance Windows:
- Primary window: Every Sunday 2:00 AM – 4:00 AM UTC
- Secondary window (if needed): Wednesday 3:00 AM – 4:00 AM UTC
- Maximum downtime per window: 60 minutes

Maintenance Notification Policy:
- Routine maintenance: 48-hour advance notice via email and in-app banner
- Emergency maintenance: Minimum 2-hour notice (when possible) via email + SMS (if opted in)
- Cancellation: If maintenance is cancelled, a cancellation notice is sent 30 minutes before the start of the window
- Subscribe to maintenance notifications at status.campaignmanager.io

What happens during maintenance:
- Campaign serving continues uninterrupted in most cases (serving layer is separate from management layer)
- Dashboard and API may be unavailable for read/write operations
- Any scheduled report exports are queued and processed immediately after maintenance ends
- Webhook deliveries are queued and delivered after maintenance completion

=======================================================
SECTION 4: INCIDENT RESPONSE & COMMUNICATION
=======================================================

Incident Severity Levels:
- P0 (Critical): Complete platform outage or campaign serving failure affecting 10%+ of users
  - On-call engineer paged immediately; initial response within 15 minutes
  - Status page updated every 15 minutes during active incident
  
- P1 (High): Significant degradation affecting a major feature (e.g., analytics unavailability, reporting failures)
  - Response within 30 minutes
  - Status page updated every 30 minutes
  
- P2 (Medium): Minor feature degradation affecting some users
  - Response within 2 hours
  - Status page updated hourly

- P3 (Low): Individual user-reported issues, no widespread impact
  - Response within 8 business hours

Communication Channels During Incidents:
- Status Page: status.campaignmanager.io (real-time) — subscribe for instant push notifications
- Email: Incident emails sent to the registered notification contacts
- SMS: Opt-in at Settings > Notifications > SMS Alerts
- Enterprise Slack channel: Dedicated #incidents channel for Enterprise customers
- Twitter/X: @CM_Status for major incident updates

Root Cause Analysis (RCA):
- P0/P1 incidents: Full RCA shared within 72 hours of incident resolution
- RCAs include: timeline of events, root cause, impact assessment, remediation steps taken, future prevention measures
- Access all historical RCAs at status.campaignmanager.io/incidents

=======================================================
SECTION 5: DISASTER RECOVERY
=======================================================

Data Backup Policy:
- Campaign configuration data: Backed up every 4 hours; recovery point objective (RPO) = 4 hours
- Analytics and reporting data: Backed up daily; retained for 13 months
- Creative assets: Stored redundantly in 3 geographically separated data centres

Recovery Time Objective (RTO):
- Critical systems (campaign serving): RTO = 30 minutes
- Management dashboard: RTO = 2 hours
- Historical analytics: RTO = 4 hours

Geographic Redundancy:
- Platform hosted across 3 AWS regions (US-East, EU-West, AP-Southeast)
- Automatic failover to secondary region triggers when primary region capacity falls below 30%
- Enterprise customers can request data residency in specific regions (EU, US, or APAC)
